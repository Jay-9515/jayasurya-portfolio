Reverse Engineering of Rod Cap (Connecting Rod)

Complete 3D scanning and CAD reconstruction workflow

Project Images

Complete workflow from original part to CAD model

3D scanned part with high detail capture

Final parametric CAD model in Fusion 360

Project Overview

This project demonstrates a complete reverse engineering workflow of a connecting rod cap component. The process involves scanning the connecting rod using a Scantech 3D scanner, converting the scanned data to a mesh format, redefining the origin, and then reconstructing the part back to its original CAD model form. After obtaining accurate dimensions from the scan, the part was reverse-engineered to create a final, production-ready CAD model of the connecting rod cap.

Project Location: University of Luxembourg

Process Workflow

1
3D Scanning

Used Scantech 3D scanner to capture high-resolution point cloud data of the physical connecting rod cap. This created a digital representation with sub-millimeter accuracy.

2
Mesh Processing

Converted point cloud to polygon mesh and refined the surface geometry. Redefined origin for proper alignment and optimized mesh topology for efficient CAD reconstruction.

3
CAD Reconstruction

Created parametric CAD model in Fusion 360 with precise dimensions retrieved from scan data. The final model is manufacturing-ready with fully editable features.

Technologies and Tools Used

Scantech 3D Scanner Fusion 360 3D Meshing Software CAD Modeling

Key Achievements

  • Successfully captured high-precision 3D scan data with sub-millimeter accuracy
  • Optimized mesh topology for efficient CAD reconstruction workflow
  • Created manufacturing-ready parametric model with fully editable features
  • Reduced traditional measurement time by implementing digital reverse engineering workflow
  • Successfully reconstructed original connecting rod cap geometry from scan data

Bell Crank Optimization with Ansys Workbench and Fusion 360

Generative design optimization for lightweight structural component

Project Overview

In order to optimize a solid body into a bell crank, a solid body was created in accordance with basic design considerations, taking into account the loads as needed. The design utilized the material properties of LUVOCOM 3F PET CF 9780 BK, a high-performance carbon fiber reinforced PET material.

The component was regenerated using Fusion 360 Generative Design, achieving the lowest possible weight of approximately 53 grams while maintaining structural integrity under specific load conditions. The optimized design achieved a maximum displacement of 0.383 mm with a factor of safety of 2.8, buckling factor of 1, and maximum stress of 119.54 MPa corresponding to the load on the body.

Project Location: University of Luxembourg

Design & Optimization Process

1
Initial Design

Created solid body in accordance with basic design considerations. Analyzed load requirements and established design constraints for the bell crank component.

2
Material Selection

Selected LUVOCOM 3F PET CF 9780 BK - a high-performance carbon fiber reinforced PET material with excellent strength-to-weight ratio and mechanical properties suitable for demanding applications.

3
Generative Design Optimization

Used Fusion 360 Generative Design to explore hundreds of design alternatives. The AI-driven process optimized for minimum weight while maintaining structural performance requirements.

4
FEA Validation

Performed comprehensive structural analysis using Ansys Workbench to validate the optimized design. Verified stress distribution, displacement, factor of safety, and buckling behavior under applied loads.
